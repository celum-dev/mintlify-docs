VirtCloud organizations let you collaborate with your team by granting each member a role that determines what they can see and do. Whether you're onboarding a new engineer or giving a stakeholder read-only visibility, role-based access keeps your infrastructure secure and your team productive.

## Roles overview

Every organization member is assigned one of three roles. Permissions are cumulative from Viewer upward — higher roles include all capabilities of lower ones.

| Role       | Capabilities |
|------------|--------------|
| **Owner**  | Full access to all resources and settings. Manages billing, can delete the organization, and can transfer ownership to another Admin. Only one Owner per organization. |
| **Admin**  | Creates, deletes, and manages all resources. Invites and removes team members. Cannot manage billing or delete the organization. |
| **Viewer** | Read-only access to all resources. Cannot create, modify, or delete any resource. Useful for auditors and stakeholders. |

## Inviting a team member

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Team Settings">
    In the VirtCloud dashboard, navigate to **Settings → Team**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the invitation">
    Click **Invite Member** in the top-right corner of the Team page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter details">
    Type the invitee's email address and select the role you want to assign — **Admin** or **Viewer**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the invite">
    Click **Send Invite**. The invitee receives an email with a link to join your organization.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Invitations expire after 7 days. If a teammate doesn't receive the email, check their spam folder or resend the invite from the **Pending Invites** section of the Team page.
</Note>

## Changing a member's role

Go to **Settings → Team**, click the member whose role you want to update, then select **Change Role** and choose the new role. The change takes effect immediately — no re-login required for the affected user.

## Removing a member

Go to **Settings → Team**, click the member you want to remove, then select **Remove from Organization** and confirm.

<Note>
  Removing a member immediately revokes all API keys that belong to their account. Any automated processes or integrations relying on those keys will stop working. Make sure to rotate or reassign those integrations before removing the member.
</Note>

## Accepting an invitation

When you're invited to a VirtCloud organization, you receive an email containing a unique join link. Click the link to accept the invitation.

- If you already have a VirtCloud account registered to that email address, you are added to the organization automatically after confirming.
- If you don't have an account yet, you are prompted to create one during the acceptance flow. Your new account is linked to the organization immediately upon creation.

## API key ownership

API keys in VirtCloud belong to individual users, not to the organization. Each user generates and manages their own keys under **Settings → API Keys**. When a member is removed from the organization, their API keys are revoked and cease to function — even if those keys were used to provision shared resources. Plan key ownership carefully for long-running automation or CI/CD pipelines.

## Transferring ownership

Only the current Owner can transfer ownership, and the recipient must already be an Admin in the organization.

Go to **Settings → Team**, click the Admin you want to promote, then select **Transfer Ownership** and confirm. The previous Owner is automatically downgraded to the Admin role and remains in the organization.
